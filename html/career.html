So now are you ready to explore your new teaching career?

What are we looking for?

In line with our core values, we look for the following traits in our teachers:

  • A University degree - This must be a 3 year Bachelor degree or higher
  • A native-level of English (native level master)
  • A qualiﬁcation in English Language Teaching (Cambridge CELTA, Trinity TESOL or an equivalent Level 5 qualiﬁcation preferred). *Your certiﬁcation must be from a recognised provider, a minimum of 120 hours in-class and have at least 4 hours of real teaching practice with authentic students.
  • A growth mind-set, i.e. open to change, learning and growth.
  • Open-mindedness, i.e. culturally sensitive and tolerant, willing to try new things and meet new people.
  • Professionalism, i.e. strong work ethic, conscientious and accountable.

Type of classes

ILA offers classes to all ages and levels, but our YL (Young Learner) classes are the most popular. Around 80% of our classes are for students between the age of 3 to 17. As well as YL classes we teach Adults, Exam classes and Corporate classes.

Working schedule

As is common in the TEFL industry, most of our teachers work at evenings and weekends, apart from public schools and corporate classes. Weekends are our busiest time with 5 classes during a day ranging from 7am to 9pm. Teachers working on verage 6 to 8 hours a day on weekends. During the week it’s less busy, with most teachers working either 2 or 4 hours, starting from around 4 pm. Teachers can have two or three days off per week but not necessarily consecutively

Work permits

If your application is successful, you will be contacted by a member of our recruitment staff. Please note, because of government regulations regarding work permits, you will need to provide the following:

  • 1. The original copy of your University Bachelor's degree. This document must be authenticated.
  • 2. The original copy of your TEFL/TESOL certificate will be required.
  • 3. The original copy of your clean police clearance certificate from your home country. If you don't already have such a certificate, you can look into getting one and arrange to have it sent to our address at ILA.
  • 4. It is important that the names on your degree and criminal records check are identical. Please also note that your police check must be dated within 6 months of the application date for your work permit.This document must be legalized at the Vietnamese embassy in your home country and will be retained by the Vietnamese government.

If you have already been in Vietnam for over 6 months, a local VNPCC (Vietnamese Police check) will be required

* Please note depending on where you are placed for work, there may be regional variations to documentation requirements.
